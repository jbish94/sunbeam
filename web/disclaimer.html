Medical Disclaimer

Effective date: June 12, 2026

Please read this disclaimer carefully before using Sunbeam.

Not Medical Advice

Sunbeam is a general wellness and tracking tool. It is not a medical device, and nothing in the app — including UV index readings, suggested sun windows, exposure recommendations, vitamin D references, or safety tips — constitutes medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. The app's recommendations are generalized estimates and are not tailored to your complete medical situation.

Sun Exposure Carries Real Risks

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun causes sunburn, premature skin aging, eye damage, and skin cancer, including melanoma. No app can determine a "safe" amount of sun exposure for you. Individual risk varies widely based on skin type, family history, medications, medical conditions, altitude, reflection from surfaces, and other factors the app cannot measure.

Data May Be Inaccurate

UV index and weather information come from third-party sources and are estimates for your general area. Actual UV exposure at your exact location can differ significantly. Do not rely on the app as your only basis for sun protection decisions.

Consult a Professional

Talk to a qualified healthcare professional before making decisions about sun exposure, especially if you:

  • have a personal or family history of skin cancer;
  • have fair or sensitive skin, many moles, or a history of severe sunburns;
  • take medications that increase sun sensitivity (for example, certain antibiotics, retinoids, or diuretics);
  • have a condition affected by sunlight, such as lupus or photosensitivity;
  • are seeking advice about vitamin D — supplements may be a safer option for many people.

Emergencies

If you experience severe sunburn, blistering, heat exhaustion, heat stroke symptoms, or any other medical emergency, seek medical care immediately. Do not rely on this app in an emergency.

Your Responsibility

By using Sunbeam, you acknowledge that you use the app's information at your own risk and that you remain responsible for your own sun protection choices, including the use of sunscreen, protective clothing, shade, and limiting exposure.
